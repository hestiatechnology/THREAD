THREAD does not define new standards — it implements existing open standards and fills the operational gaps between them. This page documents how THREAD relates to each relevant standard and what that means in practice.

## GS1 Digital Link

**What it is:** A standard for encoding GS1 product identifiers (GTIN, batch/lot, serial) as HTTPS URLs that resolve to product data.

**THREAD alignment:** GS1 Digital Link is the mandatory identifier format for all THREAD DPPs. The `dpp.id` field must be a valid GS1 Digital Link URI. The QR code generated by THREAD encodes this URI.

**Why it matters:** The EU ESPR draft guidance recommends GS1 Digital Link as the carrier technology. Adopting it now ensures forward compatibility.

**Reference:** [gs1.org/standards/gs1-digital-link](https://www.gs1.org/standards/gs1-digital-link)

---

## GS1 EPCIS 2.0

**What it is:** Electronic Product Code Information Services — a GS1 standard for sharing supply chain event data (what happened, where, when, to what product or batch).

**THREAD alignment:** THREAD's `manufacturing[]` array maps to EPCIS `TransformationEvent` records. Brands and suppliers who already use EPCIS 2.0 can submit their event stream to THREAD via the API; the adapter layer maps EPCIS events to THREAD's manufacturing schema.

**EPCIS event → THREAD field mapping:**

| EPCIS field | THREAD field |
|---|---|
| `eventTime` | `batch.productionDate` |
| `readPoint.id` | `manufacturing[].facility.id` |
| `bizStep` | `manufacturing[].stage` |
| `inputEPCList` | `materials[].supplierRef` |
| `outputEPCList` | `dpp.id` |

**Reference:** [gs1.org/standards/epcis](https://www.gs1.org/standards/epcis)

## UN Transparency Protocol (UNTP)

**What it is:** A UN/CEFACT initiative defining open protocols for supply chain transparency, including a Digital Product Passport schema, Digital Traceability Events, and Digital Conformity Credentials using W3C Verifiable Credentials.

**THREAD alignment:** THREAD's schema is structurally compatible with the UNTP DPP specification. Key alignments:
- THREAD's `provenance[]` array implements UNTP's Digital Conformity Credential concept
- W3C Verifiable Credentials are accepted as `evidenceRef` values in provenance entries
- THREAD's batch traceability model aligns with UNTP's Digital Traceability Event structure

**Current status:** UNTP is still in draft (as of 2025–2026). The textile extension is not yet finalised. THREAD will adopt UNTP Digital Conformity Credentials as a first-class evidence type when the spec matures.

**Reference:** [uncefact.github.io/spec-untp](https://uncefact.github.io/spec-untp/)

## Textile Exchange Standards

**What they are:** Industry standards for responsible fibre and material sourcing — Recycled Claim Standard (RCS), Global Recycled Standard (GRS), Organic Content Standard (OCS), and the Preferred Fiber & Materials Market Report.

**THREAD alignment:** Textile Exchange certification codes are valid values in `materials[].certifications`:

| Code | Standard | Issued by |
|---|---|---|
| `RCS` | Recycled Claim Standard | Textile Exchange |
| `GRS` | Global Recycled Standard | Textile Exchange |
| `OCS` | Organic Content Standard | Textile Exchange |
| `OE100` | Organic Exchange 100 Standard | Textile Exchange |

**Reference:** [textileexchange.org/standards](https://textileexchange.org/standards/)

---

## GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard)

**What it is:** The leading certification standard for organic fibres, covering the entire supply chain from raw material to finished product.

**THREAD alignment:** GOTS certification is a valid value in `materials[].certifications` and `social.certifications`. GOTS-certified products must disclose the GOTS certificate number, which is stored in the `evidenceRef` field of the relevant provenance entry.

GOTS maintains a public database of valid certificates at `global-standard.org/public-database`. THREAD's validation engine can optionally verify certificate validity against this database.

**Code:** `GOTS`

## OEKO-TEX Standards

**What they are:** A family of independent testing and certification standards for textile safety and sustainability.

**THREAD alignment:**

| OEKO-TEX Standard | THREAD field | Code |
|---|---|---|
| STANDARD 100 by OEKO-TEX | `materials[].certifications` | `OEKOTEX-100` |
| MADE IN GREEN by OEKO-TEX | `social.certifications` | `OEKOTEX-MIG` |
| LEATHER STANDARD | `materials[].certifications` | `OEKOTEX-LEATHER` |
| STeP by OEKO-TEX (facility) | `manufacturing[].certifications` | `OEKOTEX-STEP` |

## Higg Index (SAC)

**What it is:** A suite of tools developed by the Sustainable Apparel Coalition for measuring environmental and social performance across the apparel and footwear supply chain. The Higg Facility Environmental Module (FEM) is widely used for facility-level carbon and water data.

**THREAD alignment:** Higg FEM is a recognised methodology for `environmental.carbonFootprint.methodology`. When the methodology is `"Higg FEM"`, the `evidenceRef` should reference the Higg FEM assessment ID.

**Codes:**

| Value | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `"Higg MSI"` | Material Sustainability Index |
| `"Higg FEM"` | Facility Environmental Module |
| `"Higg FSLM"` | Facility Social & Labor Module |

## Fair Trade

**What it is:** A certification system for fair wages, safe working conditions, and community development in global supply chains.

**THREAD alignment:** Fair Trade certification is valid in `social.certifications`:

| Code | Standard |
|---|---|
| `FAIRTRADE` | Fairtrade International |
| `FTC` | Fair Trade Certified (Fair Trade USA) |

---

## REACH (EU Chemical Regulation)

**What it is:** The EU regulation on Registration, Evaluation, Authorisation and Restriction of Chemicals. SVHC (Substances of Very High Concern) disclosure is required under REACH Article 33.

**THREAD alignment:** The `compliance.reachSVHC` array is the mechanism for REACH SVHC disclosure in a THREAD DPP. An explicit declaration is always required — an empty array means no SVHCs were detected; the field must not be omitted.

---

## ZDHC MRSL

**What it is:** The Zero Discharge of Hazardous Chemicals Manufacturing Restricted Substances List — an industry-wide list of hazardous chemicals that should not be intentionally used in textile and footwear manufacturing.

**THREAD alignment:** The `compliance.zdhcMRSL` field accepts:

| Value | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `"compliant"` | Facility has confirmed compliance with ZDHC MRSL |
| `"non-compliant"` | Non-compliance identified |
| `"not-assessed"` | ZDHC MRSL status has not been evaluated |
